What is a Chatbot?

A chatbot is software that converses with users through natural language — text or voice — to answer questions, complete tasks, or guide decisions.

Modern chatbots don't just match keywords; many use large language models (LLMs) to understand intent and generate human-like replies.

History of Chatbots · Origins 1964–1999

Rules & Scripts

1966
ELIZA (MIT, Joseph Weizenbaum)

Pattern matching + keyword rules. The DOCTOR script simulated a Rogerian therapist — created the illusion of understanding, not real comprehension.

1972
PARRY

Simulated a paranoid patient. Used in early Turing Test experiments alongside ELIZA.

1995
ALICE (Richard Wallace)

AIML markup language; 50,000+ pattern categories. Open-source and hobbyist-friendly.

Key idea: conversation via scripts and rules, not learning from data.

History of Chatbots · Web & mobile era 2001–2019

Assistants Go Mainstream

2001
SmarterChild (AIM, MSN, Yahoo)

First mass-market chatbot — 30M+ users. Human-curated responses: accurate but inflexible.

2011
IBM Watson · Siri

Watson won Jeopardy! Siri brought voice assistants to phones; Alexa (2014) and Google Assistant (2016) followed.

2016
Messenger bots

Businesses automated FAQs, orders, and support on platforms billions already used daily.

Key idea: useful assistants, but narrow intents and shallow dialogue.

History of Chatbots · LLM era 2020–today

Language Models Take Over

  • Modern chatbots are powered by large language models (LLMs), not hand-written scripts
  • ChatGPT (2022) made conversational AI mainstream
  • This course: LLM + RAG so answers stay grounded in your data

Next: what LLMs are, why they were built, and how they changed chatbots.

What is a Large Language Model?

A large language model (LLM) is a deep neural network trained on massive amounts of text to predict the next token (a word piece).

  • Tokens — text is split into sub-word units; the model picks the most likely next token, again and again
  • "Large" — billions of parameters + huge training corpora (books, web, code)

Think of it as autocomplete at extraordinary scale — not a database of canned replies.

Why Were LLMs Created?

Old limits

  • Rule-based — breaks on unexpected phrasing
  • Intent + slots — only predefined tasks (Siri era)
  • Small neural LMs — weak open-ended chat

What researchers wanted

  • One model, many tasks
  • Longer context — remember earlier turns
  • Pre-train once, adapt via prompts

History of LLMs · Before the Transformer 1990s–2016

Statistics, Then Neurons

  • 1990s–2000s — Statistical models (n-grams): predict next word from word counts
  • 2010s — Word embeddings (Word2Vec), RNNs/LSTMs: neural but slow to train
  • 2013–2016 — Seq2seq + attention: models learn to "look back" at earlier words

Language modeling existed for decades — the 2017 Transformer changed how we scale it.

2017 — The Transformer

"Attention Is All You Need"

Vaswani et al., Google

  • Self-attention — every word can relate to every other word in parallel
  • GPT uses decoder stacks; BERT uses encoder-only stacks

History of LLMs · GPT era 2018–today

From GPT-1 to Today

2018
GPT-1 — pre-train on text, then fine-tune for tasks
2019
GPT-2 — coherent long-form text; responsible-release debate
2020
GPT-3 — 175B parameters; few-shot prompting demos
2022
ChatGPT — RLHF + chat UI; ~100M users in two months
Today
GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, LLaMA, Mixtral — Groq hosts open models (this course)

How LLMs Power Chatbots

User message→ Tokenize→ LLM generates→ Reply
  • Strengths — open-ended Q&A, summarization, tone via system prompts
  • Risks — hallucinations, outdated knowledge, cost/latency

What Chatbots Help Us With · For businesses (1/2)

Speed, Scale, Cost

  • 24/7 support — answers without waiting for business hours
  • Scale — thousands of conversations at once
  • Cost efficiency — automate FAQs; humans handle complex cases
  • Faster answers — order status, passwords, product info instantly

What Chatbots Help Us With · For businesses (2/2)

Consistency & Routing

  • Consistency — same quality on every interaction
  • Self-service — no phone trees or long hold times
  • Routing & triage — escalate the right issues to the right team

What Chatbots Help Us With · Across industries

Everywhere You Look

  • Education — tutoring, syllabus/FAQ bots, simulated-patient practice for medical students
  • Healthcare — scheduling, reminders, symptom triage, mental health check-ins
  • HR — onboarding, policy FAQs, leave and benefits questions
  • Legal / compliance — document Q&A and policy summaries (with disclaimers)
  • E-commerce — returns, tracking, product recommendations

Best systems combine AI + human review + grounded data (RAG) — especially in healthcare and legal contexts.

Rule-based vs LLM-powered

Rule-based

  • Predictable, fast, cheap
  • Breaks on unexpected input
  • Hard to maintain at scale

LLM-powered

  • Flexible, natural language
  • Can hallucinate wrong facts
  • Needs guardrails + retrieval (RAG)

High-Level Pipeline

User→ Input→ Processing→ Response

Every chatbot — simple or advanced — follows this flow

Inside "Processing"

  • Understand — parse the user's message (intent, entities)
  • Decide — rules, retrieval, or LLM generation
  • Generate — craft a reply (template or model output)
  • Return — send text back to the user interface

Later weeks add retrieval (RAG) before the LLM step so answers stay grounded in your data.
